Scientific Rationale
🧠 Periodization Model

This plan uses linear periodization — Phase 1 (Wk 1–4) builds neuromuscular foundations at lower intensity; Phase 2 (Wk 5–8) increases volume and complexity. Youth athletes benefit from this structure as it allows the nervous system to adapt before adding load, reducing injury risk.

🎯 Core Stability First

Research (McGill, 2010; NSCA Youth Guidelines) shows that a stable core is prerequisite for efficient force transfer during basketball movements — cutting, jumping, landing. Core work is placed early in each session when the CNS is fresh, maximizing motor learning quality.

⚡ Agility Before Power

Agility drills require high cognitive demand (change of direction + reaction). Placing them before power work (while athletes are mentally sharp but physically warmed) follows the Potentiation Principle — the pattern-learning from agility primes motor pathways for explosive power output.

💥 Plyometric Safety (Youth)

NSCA recommends youth plyometrics start with low volume (40–80 foot contacts/session) and emphasis on landing mechanics before progressing to reactive work. Phase 1 focuses on controlled jumps; Phase 2 adds bounding and reactive drills only after landing quality is established.

🔄 3×/Week Frequency

For youth athletes (10–17), ACSM recommends 3 resistance/athletic training sessions per week with 48h recovery between sessions. This plan inserts seamlessly into basketball training as a 25–30 min pre-practice block, so total session time stays manageable.

📈 Progressive Overload

Each week, either reps/sets increase OR rest periods shorten OR exercise complexity advances. Only ONE variable changes per week, following the SAID Principle (Specific Adaptation to Imposed Demands) while avoiding overtraining.

Safety Guidelines

✅ Always complete full warm-up before power work
✅ Emphasize landing mechanics: soft knees, hips back, quiet feet
✅ Stop immediately if acute pain (not fatigue/burn) occurs
✅ Hydration: water break between each segment
✅ Adequate sleep (8–10h) is required for youth adaptation
✅ No max effort plyometrics on consecutive days
✅ Modify for players with known injury history
